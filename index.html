Key Components

  • Title: Clear, concise, and descriptive of your research focus
  • Abstract: Brief summary of your entire proposal (150-300 words)
  • Introduction: Background information and context for your research
  • Literature Review: Survey of existing research on your topic
  • Research Questions: Specific questions your research will answer
  • Methodology: How you plan to conduct your research
  • Timeline: Schedule for completing your research
  • Bibliography: List of sources you'll use

Crafting Strong Research Questions

Characteristics of Good Research Questions:

  • Specific - Not too broad or vague
  • Researchable - Can be investigated with available methods
  • Significant - Addresses an important issue
  • Feasible - Can be completed within your time and resource constraints

Example: "How does social media usage affect the sleep patterns of college students?"

Creating Your Research Timeline

üìÖ Sample Timeline (10-week project):

  • Weeks 1-2: Topic selection and preliminary research
  • Weeks 3-4: Literature review and source gathering
  • Weeks 5-6: Methodology development and data collection planning
  • Weeks 7-8: First draft writing and revision
  • Weeks 9-10: Final editing, formatting, and submission

Build in buffer time for unexpected challenges!

Common Pitfalls

‚ùå Avoid These Mistakes:

  • Topic too broad: "The effects of technology" vs. "Impact of smartphone usage on teenage sleep patterns"
  • Weak research questions: Avoid yes/no questions or ones that can't be researched
  • Insufficient literature review: Don't rely on just 2-3 sources
  • Unrealistic timeline: Be honest about time constraints
  • Poor methodology: Method should match your research questions

Remember: It's better to do thorough research on a narrow topic than superficial research on a broad one!
